Scope of this engagement

One framing keeps this project focused:

The boundary

This is a brand-identity project for the Preoptima masterbrand.

In scope

The Preoptima brand system — logotype, colour, typography, the core visual language, and the rules for using them.

Directional only

How CONCEPT and PACER sit beneath Preoptima. Under the single-masterbrand approach you favour (Option A), we'll show how the products are expressed under the parent — as an indicative system, not two separately developed identities.

Not this phase
  • Full, standalone identities for CONCEPT, PACER (or a future BUILDING). Distinct per-product logos with their own flexibility — the Option B direction — is a larger, separate engagement.
  • Website design or build, and product UI. The sites and references across the survey (stormtrap, Arcol, 2050-materials, Epoch) are valuable as tone and we've absorbed them — but they inform a later web phase. Nothing web-related is a deliverable here.

Why it matters: it keeps the budget and the team's energy on the one asset everything else inherits from — the parent brand.

What's already settled

From the survey, these read as decided — we'll build on them.

  • Keep the name, Preoptima. Driven by recognition, particularly with local authorities, and the cost of rebuilding awareness from zero.
  • Architecture: Option A. One Preoptima masterbrand, with CONCEPT and PACER as product names beneath it.
  • No “carbon” in the masterbrand name. Products stay free to speak to their own domains.
  • Away from today's green / blue — and away from pastel or “wishy-washy” colour.
  • Minimal on-site imagery register — noted for the future web phase.

Four decisions we need from you

These are the live splits in the survey. We'll advise on every one — you'll find where we'd start under Our recommended direction — but the calls are yours, and they gate the design.

Decision 01

Carbon or digitalisation — what does the masterbrand lead with?

The splitMichela wants to move away from “Sustainability / Carbon / Net Zero / Climate” as politicised terms, and lead on digitalisation, structured process and efficiency. Jay cautions that dropping the carbon framing sits at odds with CONCEPT and the Stage 3–4 tool, both envisioned as core carbon calculators.

Why it's firstThis isn't a tone preference — it's the spine of the identity. It decides what Preoptima means, how it's described, and the words the brand owns.

Decision 02

Colour territory — navy, or warm and earthy?

The splitA deep, trustworthy navy base with one bright accent (Michela shared a navy set with a yellow accent) versus a warm, earthy black / white / orange direction referencing Epoch Biodesign (Francesco, with Alex's strong agreement). Both agree on leaving today's green / blue behind — but these are genuinely different visual worlds.

How we'll resolve itRather than settle this by debate, we'll bring both to life as directions early, so you're choosing from something you can see. We only need your blessing to explore both.

Decision 03

Logo architecture — and Jay's point

The splitThe majority backs Option A (one masterbrand, products named beneath). Jay's preference is separate product logos, to keep the flexibility to rename and innovate.

The scope readOption A is what this engagement delivers — and it's the stronger call for a small company that needs one trusted parent both an architect and a planning officer recognise. Separate, independently flexible product identities (Option B) is a larger, later project — worth its own conversation, not something to solve inside this one. We'll design Preoptima so products slot beneath it cleanly.

Decision 04

The name — keep, but redefine

The nuanceKeeping Preoptima is settled, and the recognition argument holds. But even among those who'd keep it, several said they don't love it or find it hard to land quickly. That's not a reason to change it — it's a reason to give Preoptima a fresh meaning, deliberately, rather than carry the old carbon association forward by default. “What Preoptima stands for now” is part of the work.

Our recommended direction

Where we'd start — for you to react to, not a decision made for you.

On carbon

There's a resolution that satisfies both Michela and Jay, and it's the one your own positioning research points to: a masterbrand that doesn't name carbon, and instead leads on structured, evidence-led decisions across a building's life — “decide it early, prove it at planning.” Carbon stays alive where it belongs, inside CONCEPT and PACER. The parent stays broad enough for an Energy Compliance or Circular Economy module to sit under it without renaming anything.

On personality

The survey and the research land in the same place: authority earned through evidence, with warmth. Confident but never hyped; innovative but never an “AI shill.” Trusted because it's rigorous, not because it's loud — the tightrope both a fast-moving design team and a cautious planning officer will accept.

On colour

We'll bring both the navy and the warm / earthy routes to life — with one honest caution on each. A navy-plus-accent system needs its contrast checked so it stays accessible and doesn't read as generic corporate; and the warm / earthy route should take from Epoch Biodesign without landing so close it reads as a copy. Seeing both, against your own words, is the fastest way to know which is right.
