SNØHETTA — AUDIENCE RESEARCH NOTES
Prepared by: Sofia Berg, Head of Communications
Date: April 2026

PRIMARY AUDIENCE — INSTITUTIONAL COMMISSIONERS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Who they are:
Directors, deputy directors, and chief executives of cultural institutions (museums, libraries,
opera houses, concert halls), municipal governments (city planning departments, infrastructure
authorities), and large private developers (mixed-use, hospitality, civic-adjacent).

Budget range: $10M–$500M+ commission. Projects are typically public-facing and require
regulatory approval, community engagement, and long time horizons (5–15 years).

Where they come from:
- Referrals from previous clients and collaborators (primary channel)
- Award recognition and press coverage (Architectural Record, Dezeen, The Architectural Review)
- Academic connections (Harvard GSD, AA London, ETH Zurich)
- International architecture competitions

What they need to feel before contacting Snøhetta:
1. Trust in the firm's track record with projects of comparable scale and complexity.
2. Confidence that Snøhetta understands their specific context (civic, cultural, political).
3. A sense of the firm's values — particularly around community engagement and sustainability.
4. Proof that the firm can manage projects across geographies and cultures.

Current friction on the website:
The portfolio is project-heavy but lacks strategic framing. A museum director visiting the
site cannot easily answer: "Has Snøhetta done something like my project before? What was
the outcome?" The site lists projects but does not argue for them.

SECONDARY AUDIENCE — TALENT & PRACTITIONERS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Who they are:
Architecture and design graduates (top 10 programs globally), mid-career practitioners
seeking a firm with broader interdisciplinary scope, and experienced project leads considering
a move from BIG, Zaha Hadid, Foster + Partners, or similar.

What they want to see:
- The People page: Who works here? What are their backgrounds? Is there genuine diversity?
- The Studio/About page: What is the internal culture? How does collaboration actually work?
- The Journal/Editorial section: Does the firm have intellectual depth beyond the buildings?
- The Responsibility/Sustainability section: Is this a firm with a point of view on the world?

Key insight: Talent does not apply to firms they don't believe in. The website must
communicate values, not just projects. The Journal and People sections are critical for this.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
TERTIARY AUDIENCE — PRESS & ARCHITECTURE COMMUNITY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Who they are:
Architecture journalists, critics, bloggers, and enthusiasts. Awards juries. Academic
researchers. Students researching precedents.

What they need:
- High-resolution project imagery with proper credits and captions.
- Project data: location, year, size (m²), collaborators, awards.
- Press contact and downloadable press kits.
- A clear editorial position: what does Snøhetta think about architecture and the world?

Current gap: The site does not have a strong editorial presence. The Journal is underused —
it reads as news updates rather than thought leadership.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
CONVERSION GOAL (PRIMARY)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Desired action: "Book an initial conversation with Snøhetta's new business team."

This is not a transactional conversion. It is a considered, high-stakes decision by an
institutional client who has likely spent weeks or months considering their options.

The website's job is to eliminate doubt, not to manufacture urgency.

The CTA should feel like an invitation, not a prompt. It should appear at moments of
maximum conviction — after a powerful project story, after the Studio manifesto, at the
end of the Responsibility page.

Language candidates: "Start a conversation." / "Let's talk." / "Get in touch." / "Work with us."

What to avoid: "Get a quote." / "Request a proposal." / "Learn more." / "Book a demo."

AUDIENCE INSIGHT: THE TRUST THRESHOLD
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Snøhetta's primary clients do not make impulse decisions. They commission once per decade,
sometimes once per career. The website must function as a trust-building instrument over
multiple visits, not a single-session closer.

This means:
- Every page must be complete in itself — a visitor who only sees the Responsibility page
  should leave with a full impression of who Snøhetta is.
- The editorial voice must be consistent across all pages.
- Imagery must be exceptional. Compromised photography communicates compromised values.
- Load time and performance matter. A cultural institution's CTO will notice a slow site.
