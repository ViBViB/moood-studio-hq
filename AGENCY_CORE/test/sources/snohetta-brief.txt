COMPANY OVERVIEW
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Snøhetta is an internationally recognized interdisciplinary design practice founded in 1989
in Oslo, Norway. The firm operates across five disciplines: architecture, interior design,
landscape design, brand identity, and digital design.

Founded by Craig Dykers and Kjetil Trædal Thorsen, Snøhetta has grown from a small Norwegian
collective into a globally active studio with approximately 250 professionals across offices
in Oslo, New York, San Francisco, Copenhagen, Stockholm, and Adelaide.

The firm's name derives from the mountain Snøhetta in the Dovrefjell National Park — a symbol
of shared terrain and collective endeavor. The philosophy "What Lies Beyond Yourself" remains
the firm's north star: all projects begin from a position of collaboration, between disciplines,
between cultures, and between people and their environment.

KEY COMPLETED PROJECTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Oslo Opera House (2008) — Oslo, Norway
Landmark cultural infrastructure. Winner of the Mies van der Rohe Award (2009) and the World
Architecture Festival Award. The sloping marble roof extends to the waterfront, inviting the
public to walk across it. Considered a defining moment in Norwegian civic architecture.

Bibliotheca Alexandrina (2002) — Alexandria, Egypt
The New Library of Alexandria. A circular, tilted disc emerging from the Mediterranean — one
of the largest libraries in the world. Built to evoke the ancient Library of Alexandria and
restore it as a global center for knowledge.

National September 11 Memorial Museum Pavilion (2014) — New York, USA
A contemplative above-ground structure for the 9/11 Memorial Museum. The design channels
grief into geometry: two angled steel trellises framing the descent underground. Restrained,
deliberate, and deeply site-specific.

Times Square Reconstruction (2017) — New York, USA
A complete reimagining of the pedestrian experience in Times Square. Removed vehicle traffic
from Broadway between 42nd and 47th Streets, introduced granite plazas, and restored the
public realm to pedestrians.

King Abdulaziz Center for World Culture — Dhahran, Saudi Arabia
A cultural center for Saudi Aramco. A cluster of curved, interlocking towers clad in steel
coils, designed to foster science, arts, and cultural exchange in the Kingdom.

Under (2019) — Lindesnes, Norway
Europe's first underwater restaurant. Half-submerged into the sea, the structure doubles as
a marine research center. The 34-seat restaurant is positioned 5.5 meters below the sea surface.

DISCIPLINES & SERVICES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Architecture:
Civic, cultural, and public infrastructure at urban scale. Projects typically commissioned
by governments, municipalities, and cultural institutions. Budget range: $10M–$500M+.

Interior Design:
Hospitality, workplace, and cultural interiors. Deep integration with architecture projects
as well as standalone interior commissions.

Landscape Architecture:
Public realm design, waterfront interventions, park systems. Often tied to civic projects.

Brand Identity:
Visual identity, naming, and brand systems for cultural institutions, cities, and corporations.
Clients have included The Norwegian Government, IKEA, and various municipalities.

Digital Design:
Emerging practice within the firm. Web presence, digital experiences, and interactive
installations tied to architectural and brand projects.

BUSINESS CONTEXT & GOALS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The primary goal of the digital presence redesign is to align the website with the strategic
and visual ambition of the firm's built work. The current site is perceived internally as
outdated, project-heavy, and lacking editorial voice.

Key objectives:
1. Communicate the interdisciplinary nature of the practice — not just architecture.
2. Attract new clients in the $10M–$100M+ commission range, especially in North America
   and the Middle East.
3. Position the firm as a thought leader, not just a portfolio house.
4. Support talent acquisition: the firm seeks to attract exceptional graduates and
   experienced practitioners who align with Snøhetta's values.
5. Establish digital brand coherence across all six offices.

Success metric: A visitor should understand who Snøhetta is, what they stand for, and
why they are different within 90 seconds of landing on the homepage.

TONE & IDENTITY NOTES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Snøhetta communicates with restraint. The firm does not self-promote in the conventional
sense — the work is expected to speak. Any copy that feels like marketing or advertising
is immediately rejected internally.

The preferred register: calm authority. The voice is that of a senior practitioner who has
nothing to prove and a great deal to share. Sentences are long enough to carry weight, short
enough to land cleanly.

Words the firm uses: collaboration, interdisciplinary, landscape, threshold, shared, terrain,
beyond, civic, open.

Words to avoid: innovative, world-class, award-winning (even when true), solutions, seamless,
cutting-edge, transformative.

Color associations (internal): white, raw concrete, matte black, warm stone. The firm is
Scandinavian in sensibility — not minimal for aesthetics, but minimal because restraint is
a form of respect.
