Pressure Mapping: Evidence Across Every Body Type

University of Pittsburgh + Anthros Internal Pressure Studies

Long before we partnered with the University of Waterloo, our cushion technology went through one of the most rigorous pressure-mapping evaluations ever conducted on office chairs.

At the University of Pittsburgh’s Human Engineering Research Laboratories, Anthros was compared to leading chairs (Herman Miller, Steelcase, Secretlab, Haworth, etc.) using ISO-standard pressure mapping. Here’s what the data revealed:

  • Lowest peak pressure of all chairs tested

  • Most even pressure distribution (no localized hotspots)

To confirm these results, we pressure-mapped real users internally across multiple body types and sizes. Those findings? Even more compelling:

  • Lowest average peak pressure: 73.7 mmHg

  • Smallest range of pressure: 63–80.9 mmHg

A tight range matters because it means Anthros performs consistently, not only for ideal body types, but for everyone.

Translation: Less pressure. Less pain. More circulation. More comfort. All day long.

University of Waterloo Posture Testing

Posture Findings (Real Humans, Real Work)

To evaluate how Anthros performs with real people during real work, the University of Waterloo conducted a comprehensive study comparing Anthros to the Herman Miller Gaming Embody. Sixteen adults performed one full hour of computer work.

Pelvis Support

Anthros reduced posterior pelvic tilt by 2°-4°. This means users sat more upright, more aligned, and closer to neutral spine than in the Embody.
Why this matters: Posterior pelvic tilt = slouching → lumbar strain → early fatigue. Reducing it keeps the spine healthy and upright with less effort.

Thoracic Spine

Users had 4–6° less upper-back flexion (slouching) in Anthros. This improved posture remained consistent across tasks, especially reading.
Why it matters: A rounded upper back (“tech neck posture”) stresses muscles and discs every single day. Anthros helps neutralize it.

Tilting Benefit

When tilted, Anthros reduced lumbar flexion, helped maintain a neutral spine, and created better “postural recovery” when returning upright.

Waterloo Seat Pressure Findings

Seat Pressure Findings (Real Humans)

  • 36 mmHg lower peak pressure vs. Embody

  • Smaller total pressure, with notably better dispersion

  • Men showed the greatest improvements due to anatomical load differences

  • Participants naturally sat more upright in Anthros, supporting healthy posture without coaching

Key Takeaway: Even with slightly smaller contact area, Anthros delivered lower pressure and higher comfort thanks to its cushion geometry and precision posture system.

Waterloo’s Final Conclusion

Pittsburgh proved that Anthros has the best pressure-relieving cushion. Waterloo proved that Anthros keeps real people upright, aligned, comfortable, and productive. Together, they form the strongest scientific validation ever completed on an office chair.

Biomechanics Meets Engineering

3D Human Modeling

Before testing chairs on people, we validated Anthros using 3D finite-element human modeling through Simuserv and posture/disc research by Wilke et al.

The models showed:

  • Reduced L4-L5 disc pressure compared to lumbar-based designs

  • Maintained disc height due to neutral pelvis positioning

  • Lower spinal loading during upright work

  • Improved muscular balance in the thoracolumbar region

When Waterloo validated these same improvements in humans one year later, we saw the evidence converge.

Modeling predicted it. Humans confirmed it. This is what true scientific validation looks like.
